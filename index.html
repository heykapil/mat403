MAT403 - Complex Analysis I

Basic Information

Class: Mon to Fri, LH2 Timetable

Instructor: Kapil Chaudhary

Email: kapilchaudhary@gujaratuniversity.ac.in

Office: RR3 or RR4

Textbook: Brown, J. W., & Churchill, R. V. (2009). Complex variables and applications (8th ed.). McGraw-Hill. Link, Solutions

Supplementary readings:

  • Orloff, J. Lecture notes: Complex variables with applications: Mathematics. MIT OpenCourseWare. Link
  • John Conway, Functions of One Complex Variable, Springer-veriag, 1978.

Pre-requisites: None.

Topics to be covered: Unit IV from the syllabus below.

  • Unit I: Basic Definitions and notations, Algebraic properties, Polar coordinates and Euler’s formula, Products and Quotients in exponential form. Roots of complex Numbers. Continuous complex functions. Limits Involving Point at Infinity.

  • Unit II: Differentiable complex functions, Cauchy Riemann equations. Harmonic functions of two variables, Reflection principle.

  • Unit III: Elementary functions, Contours, Contour integrals, Anti-derivatives.

  • Unit IV: Cauchy-Goursat theorem, Simply connected domain, Multiply connected domains. Cauchy’s integral formula and its Extension. Liouville’s theorem, Fundamental theorem of Algebra, Maximum moduli principle of functions.

The assigned instructors, Jalpa, Naisargi, and Jyoti, have covered the topics of Unit I through Unit III.
